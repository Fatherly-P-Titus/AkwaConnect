3. Copyright Infringement Reports

3.1 How to Report Infringement

If you believe your copyright has been infringed, send a written notice containing:

checklist Required Information

  1. Your Contact Information: Name, address, phone, email
  2. Copyright Identification: Description of copyrighted work
  3. Infringing Material: URL of infringing content
  4. Good Faith Statement: Statement of good faith belief
  5. Accuracy Statement: Statement under penalty of perjury
  6. Signature: Physical or electronic signature

3.2 Notice Format

Use this template for infringement notices:

TO: Copyright Agent, Akwa-Connect

EMAIL: copyright@akwaconnect.com


RE: Copyright Infringement Notice


1. Copyright Owner: [Your Name]

2. Contact Information: [Address, Phone, Email]

3. Copyrighted Work: [Description]

4. Infringing URL: [Exact URL]

5. Statement of Good Faith: [Statement]

6. Accuracy Statement: [Statement]

7. Signature: [Your Signature]

Date: [Date]

3.3 Where to Send Notices

3.4 Our Response

Upon valid notice, we will:

  1. Acknowledge receipt within 48 hours

  4. Notify the alleged infringer
  5. Document the action taken

4. Counter-Notification Procedure

4.1 If Content is Removed

If your content is removed due to an infringement claim, you may file a counter-notification if you believe the removal was mistaken.

4.2 Counter-Notification Requirements

Your counter-notification must include:

  • Your contact information
  • Identification of removed content
  • Statement under penalty of perjury of good faith belief
  • Consent to Nigerian jurisdiction
  • Your physical or electronic signature

4.3 Counter-Notification Process

  1. Send counter-notification to copyright@akwaconnect.com
  2. We will forward to the original complainant
  3. If no legal action within 10 business days, we may restore content
  4. We may require additional information
  5. Our decision is final

5. Repeat Infringer Policy

5.1 Three-Strikes Policy

We implement a "three-strikes" policy for copyright infringement:

Strike Action Duration
First Warning, content removal Permanent record
Second Temporary suspension (30 days) 30 days
Third Permanent ban, account deletion Permanent

5.2 Aggravating Factors

We may skip strikes for:

  • Commercial-scale infringement
  • Willful infringement
  • Multiple infringements in one notice
  • Previous warnings ignored

6. Nigerian Copyright Law

6.1 Legal Framework

This policy operates under:

  • Copyright Act (Cap C28, LFN 2004): Primary copyright law
  • Cybercrimes Act 2015: Digital infringement provisions
  • Nigerian Court Decisions: Relevant case law

6.2 Penalties Under Nigerian Law

Copyright infringement in Nigeria can result in:

  • Civil damages (actual or statutory)
  • Injunctions against further infringement
  • Impoundment of infringing materials
  • Criminal penalties including fines and imprisonment

6.3 Fair Use/Fair Dealing

Nigerian law allows "fair dealing" for:

  • Research or private study
  • Criticism or review
  • Reporting current events
  • Judicial proceedings
